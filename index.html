Thank you for agreeing to participate in this study! You and your child are helping to provide valuable new information about cleft palate speech surgeries!

If you have any questions before, during, or after the MRI, please dont hesitate to ask. You can contact your study coordinator (the person who gave you this link) at any time!

BEFORE

Before the MRI:

  • Tell your child what to expect in a way that they understand. Most parents prefer that we treat the MRI as a type of play/pretend situation (ex: talk about the MRI like a space ship, putting on your helmet, preparing for a loud take off, hearing birds chirp outside the space ship, overall making it a pretend play experience). You can choose how best to approach this with your child and let us know.
  • When explaining what an MRI is, youll want to stress the importance of sitting still as a statue while the MRI takes your picture and practice this at home. When your child hears the noise, its time to be quiet.
  • You will find a video overview below. Please watch this with your child before the MRI appointment.
  • A coloring book can also be located on this website. This coloring book goes through what will happen using child-friendly language. Please take time to read and color this book with your child. You can download and print this out. Or, you can color the book virtually on this webpage.
  • Tip: Do NOT use the words scary or afraid when talking about the MRI. When you talk about it at home, use positive words like you will be so brave or the MRI is going to be so cool. We have found that when using words with a negative association (e.g. Theres nothing to be scared about or This isnt scary!), children tend to only hear the word scary and assume they should be afraid if you even mentioned the word.

DURING

During the MRI:

  • Your child will be able to wear headphones, listen to music and/or watch a movie during the MRI. We can also hear your child the whole time if they need to tell us something.
  • There are breaks during the MRI where your child can talk but should still refrain from moving in the MRI.
  • At some MRI sites, you may have the option to be in the room with your child during the MRI. If you do site in the room with the child, we ask that you avoid asking your child questions and instead just let them know you are there by saying encouraging words to them or holding their hand. When you ask your child questions they will likely move and their talking will create motion in the image.
  • MRIs are loud machines. We will protect your child's hearing by using earplugs and headphones to minimize the noise as much as possible.
  • We find that most children enjoy this experience and can easily be talked through the process, using their knowledge and control of the situation as power. If, however, your child does not want to continue, we will end the MRI without question.

AFTER

After the MRI:

  • After the MRI is finished, the researcher will help your child out of MRI and the research team will provide your child with a certificate of bravery and compensation for participating if this was included in the study agreement.
  • The pictures taken during the study will be reviewed by the radiologist and research team.
  • Your time is so appreciated! Thank you for your participating in our study!

OTHER INFORMATION

Bring comfortable clothes for your child to wear in the MRI scanner. Suggested items are:

  • Pajamas (without any metal zippers or snaps)
  • Sweatshirts (without hoods)
  • Sweatpants
  • Comfortable long sleeve t-shirts
  • Socks
